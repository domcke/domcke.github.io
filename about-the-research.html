About our Research

Transcription factors (TFs) regulate gene expression, determining cell fates. We aim to predict TF activity in any cell, enabling intentional state transitions (e.g., from diseased to healthy). Our research combines cell culture models, genetic and epigenetic editing, single-cell genomics, and various computational approaches to achieve this goal.

Learning mechanisms of TF binding site selection

TFs bind to specific DNA sequences, but only occupy a subset of potential sites in each cell type. We study the rules governing this cell-specific binding and its effects.

Building and leveraging predictive models of TF activity

We explore possible stable cell states achievable by natural TFs, beyond just natural transitions. Mapping this landscape helps us train deep learning models to predict TF 'recipes' for intentional novel cell state transitions.

Transferability across scales and organisms

Predicting cell state transitions in patients has huge therapeutic potential. We study cell-cell interactions in complex systems to translate our dish-based findings to organisms, aiming to transfer TF activity predictions across environments and species.
